Timeline

  • 1969 - ARPANET the predecessor of the Internet, is created.
  • 1971 - Email is invented by Ray Tomlison.
  • 1974 - TCP protocol is proposed, laying the foundation for internet communication.
  • 1983 - TCP/IP becomes the standard protocol for the internet.
  • 1983 - Domain Name System (DNS) is introduced.
  • 1989 - Tim Berners-Lee proposes the World Wide Web (WWW)
  • 1990 - First web server and first web browser are created.
  • 1991 - The World Wide Web (WWW) becomes publicly available.
  • 1993 - Mosaic browser popularizes the web.
  • 1994 - Amazon is founded, helping launch large-scale online shopping.
  • 1995 - Commercial use of the Internet expands rapidly.
  • 1998 - PayPal is founded, popularizing digital payments.
  • 1998 - Google is fouded.
  • 2001 - Wikipedia launches.
  • 2004 - Facebook is founded, accelerating the social web era.
  • 2006 - Twitter launches, popularizing microblogging.
  • 2005 - YouTube launches.
  • 2007 - iPhone accelerates mobile Internet adoption.
  • 2008 - Bitcoin whitepaper is published.
  • 2009 - Bitcoin network goes live, beginning the cryptocurrency era.
  • 2010 - Instagram launches.
  • 2011 - Cloud services become mainstream for businesses and consumers.
  • 2015 - Ethereum launches, enabling smart contracts.
  • 2016 - Live streaming and creator economies grow rapidly.
  • 2020 - Remote work, video conferencing, and online collaboration surge globally.
  • 2022 - Generative AI tools become widely accessible on the Internet.
  • 2020s - AI becomes a major platform layer alongside the web, mobile, social media, cloud computing, and cryptocurrencies.
